Q: How far is Unawatuna Beach from Habibi Hostel?

A: It's a 10–15 minute walk (around 700 meters). You’ll find many shops, cafes, and bars along the way and directly on the beach.

Q: Are there other beaches nearby?

A: Yes! Jungle Beach is a 20–25 minute walk through a beautiful trail. Dalawella Beach and Dewata Surf Beach are both just 2–2.2 km away — a quick tuk-tuk or bike ride.

Q: Where exactly is the hostel located?

A: We're on Rumassala Hill, a peaceful and legendary area between two beaches. This spot is known for unique herbs, jungle views, and a calm vibe above the crowds.

Q: What types of rooms do you offer?

A: We have 6 rooms: Deluxe Twin Room, Deluxe Double Room with Front & Back Balconies, Standard Double Room, 4-Bed Female Dorm, 4-Bed Mixed Dorm

Q: Do all rooms have air conditioning?

A: All rooms except the Standard Double Room have AC. Every room has a fan.

Q: Is there WiFi in the rooms?

A: Yes, there’s WiFi in all rooms and common areas — perfect for remote workers.

Q: Are towels included?

A: Yes, towels are provided in all private rooms. Dorm guests can rent a towel if needed.

Q: Is there a place to chill or work?

A: Yes, our top floor has a chill zone with a bar — ideal for working remotely or just enjoying the jungle view.

Q: Do you offer airport or local transfers?

A: Yes, we can arrange transfers anywhere on the island, including airport pick-up and drop-off.

Q: Do you offer tours?

A: Yes! We organize custom tours around Sri Lanka and shorter day trips nearby.

Q: Is there food and drink available?

A: Yes, we have an on-site bar and restaurant with Arab-inspired dishes — currently for guests only.

Q: Do you offer laundry service?

A: Yes, we offer laundry service for our guests.

Q: What kind of travelers stay at Habibi?

A: We host solo travelers, couples, and digital nomads — mostly comfort-seeking backpackers who love a peaceful atmosphere and beautiful design.

Q: Is this a party hostel?

A: No. We don’t do loud parties, but sometimes we host spontaneous activities like yoga sessions or small social gatherings.

Q: Is the hostel family-friendly?

A: We mostly host adult travelers, but families are welcome too. Some have stayed with young kids by sharing a double bed. Feel free to message us before booking!

Q: What time is check-in and check-out?

A: Check-in is from 3:00 PM, and check-out is by 12:00 noon.

Q: Do I need to show ID?

A: Yes, a valid passport is required at check-in.

Q: Are there any restrictions?

A: We’re quite relaxed — no curfew, and no special restrictions.

Q: What makes Habibi Hostel different?

A: We’re an Arab-owned boutique hostel with a soul. Many items are handmade or brought from abroad. It’s not a typical hostel — it’s a peaceful, stylish space with real heart and hospitality.

Q: Is this a luxury hostel?

A: Yes — while we’re not “budget,” we offer luxury hostel comfort at fair prices for travelers who value atmosphere and design.

Q: Can I see what's nearby?

A: Sure! Check Around Habibi Hostel for the top places to visit in Unawatuna and Galle.

OUR STORY

Nestled in the heart of Unawatuna, Sri Lanka, our hostel has been welcoming travelers since January 2023. What started as a dream back in 2017 has become a reality, crafted with dedication and heart. Our founder, originally from palestine, saw potential in this two-story structure that had little more than a foundation. Over the years, he transformed it into the vibrant four-story haven you see today, building much of it himself and carefully curating each detail from scratch. Our hostel is more than just a place to stay—it’s a blend of cultures. The design reflects elements brought from the middle east, giving the space a unique charm that combines eastern tradition with a tropical vibe. Our restaurant, located within the hostel, serves authentic arabic cuisine that has delighted hundreds of guests, adding a taste of home for some and a taste of adventure for others. From the architecture to the cuisine, every corner of our hostel tells a story of passion, resilience, and a vision brought to life across oceans. We invite you to be part of that story.
